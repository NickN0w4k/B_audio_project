# AI Music Detection with Checkerboard Artifacts - sangarshanan

**Quelle:** https://sangarshanan.com/ (Blogpost vom 8. Januar 2026)
**Typ:** Web-Artikel

## NotebookLM-Zusammenfassung

Dieser Text untersucht die technische Herausforderung, **KI-generierte Musik** zu identifizieren, um das Werk menschlicher Künstler in einer Ära massenhafter digitaler Inhalte zu schützen. Der Autor kritisiert die Unzulänglichkeiten aktueller Schutzmechanismen und hebt stattdessen eine Methode hervor, die **mathematische Unregelmäßigkeiten**, sogenannte Schachbrett-Artefakte, in den Frequenzspektren synthetischer Klänge aufspürt. Diese spezifischen Muster entstehen zwangsläufig durch die **Architektur von Upsampling-Modellen** und lassen sich bereits mit einfachen Werkzeugen wie der Fourier-Transformation sichtbar machen. Letztlich plädiert der Beitrag für einen **nachvollziehbaren, auf Grundprinzipien basierenden Ansatz** zur Detektion, da dieser effizienter und zugänglicher ist als komplexe, rechenintensive KI-Modelle.

## Originalinhalt

Last year was crazy for AI music tools; the heavily funded ones ended up getting record deals and their usage has skyrocketed. There is AI slop everywhere now and with music its not different. Apps like Spotify have already been pushing AI music to their listeners for years, and this is only going to get worse.

Several independent researchers have developed models for detecting and tagging AI music, which is a great step in the right direction. But the industry keeps evolving, and models can always be engineered to beat this metaphorical Turing test. There are also issues with interpretability and generalization of these AI detection models.

There are tools like HarmonyCloak that claim to make music unlearnable for generative AI models while leaving no perceptible artifacts, but some people refute this claim. HarmonyClook seems to have started with the MIDI format and does not yet support raw audio formats.

Benn Jordan made videos about detecting AI music and poison-pilling music to make it unlearnable with adversarial noise — using AI to beat AI.

### Checkerboard Artifact

The most interesting work has been from Deezer: "A Fourier Explanation of AI-Music Artifacts" (ISMIR 2025's best paper). It's elegant in that the work can be reproduced locally with just a Jupyter notebook.

A Google paper from 2016 talked about "Deconvolution and Checkerboard Artifacts". A lot of generative tools use deconvolution layers for upsampling the latent representation, and when doing so, pixels sometimes receive more "contributions" from the convolution than others, creating variations in a regular pattern.

For audio, this means that the outputs of generative models will produce small frequency artifacts, i.e., peaks.

This does however depend on the model architecture, and models can always use modified upsampling mechanisms to avoid this irregular kernel overlap.

**Reproducing the fingerprint:** Calculate a basic STFT to convert the audio to frequency domain, take the average spectrum over time with a sliding window and detect peak values. Also subtract the local minima of the spectrum over the window to highlight the local variations and also remove bandwidth that is of no use (filtering [5-16 kHz]).

For a track generated by Suno, the peaks are much more periodic and really stand out with a very sharp frequency cutoff. For real tracks (e.g., "Everything in Its Right Place" by Radiohead), the peaks are relatively uniform and scattered throughout the frequency range, looking more like natural harmonic content.

Once we have these fingerprints, we can train a very simple linear classifier. Since different models have different peak patterns, we would be able to classify between models as well and technically be able to tell if a track was generated with Suno v2 or Suno v3!

**Caveat:** This idea is not fully robust — simple audio manipulations like pitch shifting can change the peak frequencies and easily break the classifier. In the future, models could also build around this by using different upsampling techniques to make these artifacts disappear.

**Key insight:** AI music artifacts are not bugs but mathematical certainties given the architecture choice, and you don't need millions of parameters to detect AI music — sometimes an STFT will do.
